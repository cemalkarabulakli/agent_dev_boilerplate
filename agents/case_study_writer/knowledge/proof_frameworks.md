# Proof Frameworks

## The Proof Ladder
- Level 1 Micro-win: Small result in first 14 days. Reduces buyer remorse.
- Level 2 Milestone: Core result tied to completing a program phase (30–90 days). Primary sales conversion tool.
- Level 3 Transformation: Full before/after change (90–365 days). Justifies premium pricing.
- Level 4 Outlier: Exceptional result. Must be labeled as not typical. Use sparingly.

## Objection Mapping
Each case study should map to one primary prospect objection:
- No audience → Client who started at zero
- Too old / too new → Client at the extreme of experience
- Different niche → Client in skeptic-heavy industry
- No time → Client working full-time while doing this
- Can't afford → Client who invested despite financial fear, ROI documented
- Not technical → Least technical client imaginable
- Results not typical → Modest but real result (the realistic baseline)

## The Typical Result Standard (Hormozi Rule)
- Never lead with the best result. Lead with the typical result.
- Build proof library: 3 typical + 2 above average + 1 outlier (labeled)
- Outlier cases that look unrealistic destroy credibility faster than they build it

## Interview Questions for Extraction
Before state: "Walk me through your exact numbers when you started."
Decision: "What almost stopped you from joining?"
Process: "Walk me through the first week — what did you actually do?"
Obstacle: "Was there a moment where you thought this might not work?"
Result: "Give me the most specific number you can. By how much, in what timeframe?"
Quote: "What would you tell someone who is where you were when you started?"
