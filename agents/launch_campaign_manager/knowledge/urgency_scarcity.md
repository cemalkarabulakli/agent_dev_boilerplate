# Urgency and Scarcity in Launch Campaigns

SOURCE: launch_campaign_manager knowledge base

---

## Core Principle

Urgency and scarcity drive conversions — but only when they are real. Manufactured urgency destroys trust, violates FTC guidelines, and reduces long-term customer quality. Every urgency element used in a launch must be enforceable and enforced.

---

## Permitted Urgency and Scarcity Tactics

### 1. Real Deadline — Cart Closes
The cart closes at a stated date and time. After the deadline:
- Sales page redirects to a "closed" or "waitlist" page
- Buy button is removed or disabled
- Price (if unchanged) is clearly unavailable until next launch

**Requirements:**
- Announce the deadline in the first open cart email
- Repeat in every subsequent email
- Show a countdown timer on the sales page (must be real — tied to the actual deadline)
- Close the cart at the stated time. No exceptions.

---

### 2. Price Increase After Deadline
The founding or launch price is lower than the evergreen price. After deadline, price rises.

**Requirements:**
- State the launch price and the future price clearly in the sales page and emails
- Enforce the price increase — do not offer the old price after deadline
- The increase must be real and permanent (or until next launch)

**Example:** "Founding member price: $2,000. After [date], price increases to $3,000."

---

### 3. Expiring Bonuses
Specific bonuses are available only for buyers who enroll before the deadline.

**Requirements:**
- Each expiring bonus is listed with a clear label: "Available for founding members only" or "Expires [date]"
- After deadline, the bonus is removed from the sales page and not delivered to late buyers
- Bonus must have real, standalone value — not "bonus" in name only

**Example:** "Free live Q&A call with [name] — only for buyers who join before [date]"

---

### 4. Limited Cohort Spots
For live cohort programs, group coaching, or done-with-you formats, real capacity limits apply.

**Requirements:**
- Spots must be genuinely limited — defined by the delivery model, coach's bandwidth, or cohort size requirements
- Do not claim "10 spots remaining" if no real limit exists
- Once spots are filled, close enrollment — do not add invisible capacity

**Example:** "This cohort is capped at 20 members to ensure personal attention. X spots remain."

---

### 5. Founding Member Pricing
First launch of a new program — early adopters get a lower price in exchange for being first and providing feedback or testimonials.

**Requirements:**
- This is a one-time price for the founding group only
- Future price must be stated and intended
- Founding member price is not offered again after launch closes

---

## Prohibited Urgency and Scarcity Tactics

### 1. Countdown Timer That Resets
A countdown timer that starts over every time a new visitor loads the page. This is deceptive and violates FTC guidelines.

### 2. False Spot Limits
Claiming "only 5 spots left" when there is no real capacity constraint.

### 3. Unenforcced Deadlines
Stating a deadline, then extending it with: "We got so many requests — we're opening the cart for one more day!" This erodes trust in every future launch.

**Exception:** A documented technical failure (payment gateway down, email delivery error) can justify a genuine extension — communicate it transparently, not as a gift but as a correction.

### 4. Fake Price Increases
Claiming a price increase and then offering the same price after the deadline.

### 5. Manufactured Social Proof Notifications
Fake "John from Austin just purchased" popups with no real purchase behind them.

### 6. Implied Scarcity Without Reality
"This offer won't be available for long" with no actual end date or condition.

---

## FTC Compliance Notes

The U.S. Federal Trade Commission (and equivalent bodies in other markets) requires:
- Any claim made in marketing must be substantiated
- "Limited time" or "limited availability" claims must be true
- Testimonials and case studies must reflect real customer experiences
- Income or results claims must be typical results (or clearly labeled as atypical)

For launches in the EU or UK: GDPR and consumer protection regulations add requirements around fair commercial practices and misleading marketing.

**Default rule:** If you cannot prove the claim is true after the deadline, do not make the claim.

## Urgency Copy Principles

**Use:** Real stakes language
- "Cart closes [day] at [time] [timezone]"
- "After [date], this price is gone"
- "The [bonus] expires when the cart closes — it will not be available after [date]"

**Avoid:** Vague, pressuring, or unsubstantiated language
- "This is a one-time opportunity" (unless it genuinely is)
- "I don't know if I'll ever offer this again" (if you plan to relaunch)
- "You might miss out forever" (overstated)

---

## Ethical Urgency Checklist

Before publishing any urgency or scarcity element, confirm:
- [ ] The deadline is real and will be enforced
- [ ] The price increase (if any) will be applied after the deadline
- [ ] The expiring bonuses will be removed from the offer after the deadline
- [ ] Spot limits reflect actual delivery capacity
- [ ] The countdown timer is tied to the real deadline (not evergreen per visitor)
- [ ] No "we're extending due to demand" planned unless there is a genuine technical reason
