# Traffic Coordination for Launch Campaigns

SOURCE: launch_campaign_manager knowledge base

---

## Traffic Strategy by Launch Phase

### Phase 0: Architecture
- Install or verify tracking pixel (Meta Pixel, Google Tag, etc.)
- Create custom audiences: email list upload, website visitors, video viewers
- Draft organic content calendar
- Define paid traffic budget and channels
- Set up retargeting campaign structures in advance (do not activate yet)

---

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch Traffic

**Goal:** Build the opt-in or registration list, warm the audience

**Organic traffic:**
- Publish 1–2 pieces of content per week aligned to PLC themes
- Short-form social content daily (reinforce mechanism, share insights)
- YouTube or podcast if applicable: mechanism-focused content published 7–10 days before cart open
- Engage with comments and DMs to build warm audience

**Paid traffic:**
- Objective: leads (opt-in or registration)
- Audiences: cold lookalike (email list or buyer list), broad interest targeting
- Creative: mechanism-focused short video (30–60s) or image with curiosity hook
- Destination: opt-in page only — NOT the sales page
- Budget: $30–$100/day (scale based on opt-in rate)
- Minimum opt-in rate before scaling paid: 25% (cold traffic)

**Traffic mix recommendation:**
- If list < 1,000: 60% paid, 40% organic
- If list 1,000–5,000: 40% paid, 60% organic
- If list > 5,000: organic-first unless a JV/affiliate launch adds new traffic

---

### Phase 2: Cart Open Traffic

**Goal:** Drive warm audiences to the sales page and cart

**Organic traffic:**
- Daily content during cart open: proof, objection handling, deadline reminders
- Stories or reels with direct CTA to sales page
- Live Q&A (optional): real-time conversion event for fence-sitters

**Paid retargeting (activate on Day 1 of cart open):**
- Audiences: opt-in/registration page visitors, VSL video viewers (75%+), email list upload, sales page visitors (who did not purchase)
- Creative types:
  - Testimonial/case study video (30–60s)
  - Deadline reminder with countdown
  - Objection-handling video or image
  - "Last chance" creative (activate Day 4–5)
- Budget: 2–3× pre-launch daily budget during cart window
- Ad frequency: cap at 3–4 per person during 5-day cart window to prevent fatigue

**Paid prospecting (optional during cart):**
- Only run if opt-in page is still live and warm enough
- Lower ROI than retargeting — prioritize retargeting budget first
- Reduce or pause cold traffic if cart CVR drops below 1%

---

### Phase 3: Cart Close Traffic

**Goal:** Maximum intensity on highest-intent retargeting audiences

**Organic traffic:**
- Final social post on close day: "Today is the last day"
- Stories: countdown to deadline
- No new content — reinforce existing messaging

**Paid retargeting:**
- Increase spend 50–100% on retargeting audiences for the final 48 hours
- Refresh creatives with deadline-specific messaging: "Cart closes tonight"
- Add sales page visitors (last 48 hours) as highest-priority retargeting audience
- Pause cold prospecting entirely

**Ad creative for close window:**
- Subject line style: "Last chance: [offer name] closes in [X] hours"
- Visual: countdown timer, deadline date, "Enrollment Closing" overlay
- Body: one line transformation promise + deadline + link

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram)
- Best for: consumer-facing high-ticket ($1,000–$10,000), B2C expert services, courses, done-with-you programs
- Pre-launch: Advantage+ Leads campaign or manual leads campaign with broad targeting
- Cart open retargeting: Manual campaign with custom audiences (not ASC — you need tight retargeting control during launch)
- Creative: short-form video (Reels-format) outperforms static in cold traffic; testimonial static/carousel works well in retargeting
- Audience: upload email list as custom audience before cart opens (4+ weeks of data needed for strong match rate)

### Google Ads
- Best for: search-intent traffic (avatar is actively searching for the solution)
- Use during cart open: branded search campaign (capture anyone searching your name or offer name)
- YouTube in-stream: awareness play, not a direct response launch driver
- Not recommended as primary launch traffic for most high-ticket launches without existing search demand

### YouTube (Organic)
- Mechanism-focused video published 10–14 days before cart open
- Drives warm, high-intent opt-ins over the launch window
- Optimized title: "[Mechanism] — How to [Transformation] Without [Main Objection]"
- End screen CTA: link to opt-in or registration page

### Email (To Existing List)
- Primary traffic driver for internal launches
- Drives the highest-quality traffic to the sales page
- Open rate and click rate are the key traffic metrics
- Segment: buyers (exclude from non-buyer sequences), high-engagers (send earlier in close window)

### Podcast (Organic)
- Publish mechanism-focused episode 7–10 days before cart open
- Mid-roll and end-roll CTA to opt-in page
- Strong for niche authority positioning; weaker as direct response traffic for short launch windows

## Traffic Diagnostics

### Pre-Launch: Opt-In Rate Below 25% (Cold Traffic)
1. Test headline — try 3 variants before changing anything else
2. Check traffic quality — is the audience actually the target avatar?
3. Check page load speed — above 3 seconds kills mobile conversions
4. Check form friction — reduce to name + email only

### Cart Open: Sales Page Traffic Low
1. Check email click rates — are emails linking to the sales page?
2. Check paid traffic delivery — are retargeting audiences loading?
3. Check audience sizes — retargeting audiences under 1,000 may not deliver at scale

### Cart Open: Sales Page Traffic High but CVR Low
1. Review VSL/webinar — hook quality and completion rate
2. Review offer — is the value stack clear and compelling?
3. Review price anchoring — is the bonus stack value stated?
4. Review guarantee — is it prominent and specific?
5. Check checkout drop-off — issue may be at payment, not the sales page

### Close Window: Low Engagement on Close Emails
1. Subject lines too soft — use exact deadline language
2. Check send times — test morning vs. evening
3. Resend to non-openers with different subject line (Day 2 only)
