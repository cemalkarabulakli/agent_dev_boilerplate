# Launch Revenue Forecasting

SOURCE: launch_campaign_manager knowledge base

---

## The Launch Revenue Formula

```
Revenue = Engaged List × Email Click Rate × Cart CVR × Price
```

Every input variable is testable and improvable. When a launch underperforms, this formula identifies which lever is broken.

---

## Input Variables

### Engaged List
Not the raw list size — the number of subscribers who are actively engaging (opening emails, clicking, consuming content).

**Proxy for engagement:**
- Use: 30-day active openers as "engaged list" estimate
- If unknown, use 30–50% of total list size as a conservative estimate

**Example:** 2,000 subscribers × 40% engagement = 800 engaged leads

---

### Email Click Rate
The percentage of email recipients who click through to the sales page.

**Benchmarks:**
- Warm, well-nurtured list: 3–7%
- Cold or unengaged list: 1–3%
- Re-activated old list: 1–2%

---

### Cart Conversion Rate (CVR)
The percentage of sales page visitors who complete a purchase.

**Benchmarks by price:**
- $497–$2,000: 1.5–4%
- $2,000–$5,000: 1–3%
- $5,000–$10,000: 0.5–2%

---

### Price
The transaction value at point of purchase.

**Note:** AOV can be higher than list price if order bumps or upsells are included.

**AOV calculation:**
```
AOV = Price × (1 + Order Bump Rate × Bump Price / Price)
```

---

## Scenario Planning Model

Use three scenarios for every launch:

### Conservative (floor)
- 50% of list engaged
- Email click rate at lower benchmark
- CVR at lower benchmark
- No order bumps

### Base (target)
- 40% of list engaged
- Email click rate at mid benchmark
- CVR at mid benchmark
- 20% order bump acceptance

### Stretch (ceiling)
- 50% of list engaged
- Email click rate at upper benchmark
- CVR at upper benchmark
- 30% order bump acceptance

## Example: $3,000 Program, 3,000 List

| Scenario | Engaged List | Click Rate | CVR | Buyers | Revenue |
|----------|-------------|-----------|-----|--------|---------|
| Conservative | 1,500 | 3% = 45 | 1.5% = 0.67 → 1 buyer/100 | ~1 buyer | $3,000 |
| **Corrected Conservative** | 1,500 | 3% | 1.5% | **~1** | **$3,000** |

**Let me redo this with a larger list:**

| Scenario | Engaged List | Click Rate | Visitors | CVR | Buyers | Revenue |
|----------|-------------|-----------|----------|-----|--------|---------|
| Conservative | 1,500 | 3% | 45 | 2% | 1 | $3,000 |
| Base | 1,500 | 5% | 75 | 3% | 2 | $6,000 |
| Stretch | 1,500 | 7% | 105 | 4% | 4 | $12,000 |

**Lesson:** For list-based launches, the math requires a meaningful list size to generate predictable revenue. With 3,000 subscribers and a $3,000 program:

| Scenario | Engaged (50%) | Click Rate | Sales Page | CVR | Buyers | Revenue |
|----------|--------------|-----------|-----------|-----|--------|---------|
| Conservative | 1,500 | 3% | 45 | 2% | 1 | $3,000 |
| Base | 1,500 | 5% | 75 | 3% | 2 | $6,000 |
| Stretch | 1,500 | 7% | 105 | 4% | 4 | $12,000 |

**With paid traffic adding 500 warm visitors:**

| Scenario | Organic Visitors | Paid Visitors | Total | CVR | Buyers | Revenue |
|----------|-----------------|--------------|-------|-----|--------|---------|
| Conservative | 45 | 500 | 545 | 1% | 5 | $15,000 |
| Base | 75 | 500 | 575 | 2% | 11 | $33,000 |
| Stretch | 105 | 500 | 605 | 3% | 18 | $54,000 |

## Breakeven Calculation (Paid Traffic Launches)

```
Breakeven ROAS = Total Revenue / Total Ad Spend

Breakeven Ad Spend = Total Revenue / Minimum Acceptable ROAS
```

**Minimum acceptable ROAS for a launch with no backend:**
- 2:1 ROAS = breakeven after refund buffer
- 3:1 ROAS = healthy launch
- 5:1 ROAS = strong launch worth scaling

**Example:**
- Revenue: $30,000
- Ad spend: $6,000
- ROAS: 5:1 → Strong launch, worthy of evergreen automation

## Close Window Revenue Split

In a well-executed launch, revenue is distributed across the launch window as follows:

| Phase | % of Total Revenue |
|-------|-------------------|
| Day 1 (cart open) | 35–45% |
| Days 2–4 (middle) | 20–30% |
| Final 48 hours (close) | 30–40% |

If close window is generating less than 20% of total revenue, the close sequence is underperforming. Check:
- Are deadline emails sending at the right times?
- Is the deadline prominent on the sales page?
- Is the deadline real and communicated repeatedly?

---

## Revenue Levers by Priority

When revenue falls short of target, work through these levers in order:

1. **Traffic volume** — Are enough people reaching the sales page?
2. **Sales page CVR** — Are visitors converting? (Headline, VSL, offer)
3. **Email click rate** — Are emails driving enough traffic to the page?
4. **Email open rate** — Are subject lines working? Is the list warm?
5. **Price and offer** — Is the value stack strong enough to justify the price?
6. **Deadline enforcement** — Is the close sequence generating urgency?
