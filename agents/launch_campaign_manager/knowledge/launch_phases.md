# Launch Phases Playbook

SOURCE: launch_campaign_manager knowledge base

---

## Phase 0: Launch Architecture

**Duration:** 2–4 weeks before pre-launch begins
**Owner:** Launch strategist

### Deliverables

- Launch model selected (see: Launch Models below)
- Unique mechanism articulated in one sentence
- Transformation promise: "I help [Avatar] achieve [Dream Outcome] in [Timeframe] without [Main Objection]"
- Offer defined: core product + bonus stack + guarantee + price
- Revenue goal set with conservative / base / stretch scenarios
- Launch calendar drafted with all phase dates
- L.O.A.D. asset audit completed — gaps identified and assigned

### Launch Architecture Decision Gate
Do not proceed to Phase 1 until:
- [ ] Unique mechanism defined
- [ ] Offer (price, bonuses, guarantee) confirmed
- [ ] List size and health assessed
- [ ] L.O.A.D. audit shows all critical assets will be ready before cart open

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch

**Duration:** 7–14 days (14 recommended for cold traffic)
**Owner:** Email + content team
**Goal:** Warm the list, build anticipation, introduce the mechanism

### Pre-Launch Objectives
1. Shift the prospect's worldview — make them see their problem differently
2. Introduce the unique mechanism as the solution
3. Build anticipation and curiosity for the offer (do not reveal price or product name yet)
4. Drive registrations if running a webinar or live training

### Pre-Launch Content Sequence (3–5 pieces)
- **PLC-1:** Opportunity / Worldview Shift — opens the loop
- **PLC-2:** Problem / Villain — names the real enemy and false solutions
- **PLC-3:** Transformation / Social Proof — case study showing mechanism at work
- **PLC-4 (optional):** Mechanism deep-dive — technical authority piece
- **PLC-5 (optional):** Q&A / Community engagement — builds relationship

### Pre-Launch Traffic
- Organic content seeded on primary channel (social, YouTube, podcast)
- Paid traffic to opt-in or registration page (NOT to sales page yet)
- JV partners activated (if applicable) — send to co-registration page
- Retargeting pixel fires on all pre-launch pages

### Pre-Launch Exit Criteria
- Minimum 3 PLC emails sent and opened by >20% of list
- Webinar/registration page live (if applicable)
- Retargeting audiences seeded with >1,000 unique visitors
- Sales page and checkout tested and confirmed live

## Phase 2: Cart Open

**Duration:** 3–7 days (5 recommended)
**Owner:** Email + paid media team
**Goal:** Convert engaged prospects into buyers

### Cart Open Day 1
- Send announcement email to full list (buyers excluded or segmented)
- Social media announcement post
- Activate retargeting ads to pre-launch audiences
- Target: 40–60% of final launch revenue generated on Day 1

### Cart Open Days 2–4
- Daily email alternating between: objection handling, proof, FAQ, testimonials
- Paid traffic continues on warm audiences
- Monitor daily: open rate, click rate, cart page CVR, checkout CVR
- Diagnose and fix any conversion drop-off

### Cart Open Day 5 (or final day before close)
- Send deadline reminder email (48–72 hours before close)
- Begin close sequence

### Cart Open Exit Criteria
- Email sequence complete
- Cart page CVR tracked and diagnosed
- Close sequence loaded and ready

---

## Phase 3: Cart Close

**Duration:** Final 24–48 hours
**Owner:** Email + paid media team
**Goal:** Capture fence-sitters with real deadline pressure

### Close Window Strategy
- Close window drives 30–40% of total launch revenue
- 3-email sequence: 48hr warning → Final day → Last hour
- Paid ad spend increases on retargeting (highest intent audience)
- Subject lines focus on: deadline, loss of bonuses, price increase
- No new objection handling — deadline and loss are the only message

### Deadline Enforcement
- Cart closes at the stated time — no extensions unless documented technical failure
- Countdown timer on sales page is real and matches email deadlines
- Price increase (if promised) activates immediately after deadline
- Bonus expiry (if promised) removes bonuses from the offer page

### Close Window Exit
- Cart officially closed (page redirected or updated)
- Post-launch email to non-buyers (optional): "Cart is closed. Join waitlist."
- Buyer onboarding sequence activated

## Phase 4: Post-Launch Debrief

**Duration:** Within 72 hours of cart close
**Owner:** Launch strategist
**Goal:** Extract learnings to improve the next launch

### Debrief Metrics to Capture
- List size at launch start
- Number of opt-ins / registrations during pre-launch
- Opt-in rate (cold traffic / warm list)
- Webinar show rate (if applicable)
- VSL or webinar replay completion rate
- Total email opens and click rates per sequence
- Cart page unique visitors and CVR
- Checkout page CVR (visitors to purchasers)
- Number of buyers
- Total revenue, AOV, RPL (revenue per lead)
- Total ad spend and ROAS

### Debrief Questions
1. What was the single biggest constraint on revenue?
2. Which email had the highest conversion impact?
3. Which phase underperformed — and why?
4. What creative/offer/bonus drove the most conversions?
5. What would we test differently next time?

### Debrief Output
- Save as long-term memory: launch benchmarks, winners, failures
- Update offer based on objections heard in sales calls and email replies
- Draft improvement hypothesis for next launch

## Launch Models

### Internal Launch
- Launch to your own email list
- Minimum list: 500 engaged subscribers
- Typical revenue range: 1–3% of list size × price
- Best for: new offer validation, existing audience monetization

### Seed Launch
- Pre-sell before the product is built
- Maximum 20–30 founding members
- Use for: first launch of a new program, proof collection
- Price: 30–50% discount for founding members in exchange for feedback and testimonials

### Evergreen Launch
- Automated launch running continuously to new traffic
- Requires: proven conversion rates from at least one live launch
- Use for: scaling a validated offer with paid traffic

### JV / Affiliate Launch
- Partners promote to their list on a revenue share (typically 30–50%)
- Requires: proven sales page CVR, tested email swipes, affiliate portal
- Best for: rapid list and revenue growth with existing social proof

### PLF-Style Launch
- 3–4 pre-launch content pieces (video or email) over 10–14 days
- High-touch, story-driven, mechanism-led
- Best for: high-ticket programs ($2,000+) with warm audiences
