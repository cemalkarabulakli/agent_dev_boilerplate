# Launch Email Sequences

SOURCE: launch_campaign_manager knowledge base

## Pre-Launch Content (PLC) Sequence

### Purpose
Shift the prospect's worldview, introduce the unique mechanism, and build anticipation before the offer is revealed. Do not pitch the offer in PLC emails.

### PLC-1: The Opportunity / Worldview Shift

**Goal:** Open a curiosity loop. Make the reader see their problem from a new angle.

**Structure:**
1. Hook: A counterintuitive insight, bold claim, or surprising question
2. Worldview shift: "The reason [common approach] doesn't work is [new frame]"
3. Introduce the mechanism (name it, but do not explain it fully yet)
4. Tease PLC-2: "Tomorrow I'm going to show you the real reason most [avatars] struggle with [pain]"

**Tone:** Story-driven, curious, non-salesy
**Length:** 300–600 words
**Subject line styles:**
- "The [number] mistake that's costing [avatars] [outcome]"
- "Why [common belief] is wrong (and what actually works)"
- "I need to tell you something about [topic]"

---

### PLC-2: The Problem / Villain

**Goal:** Name the real problem and the false solutions. Deepen pain. Validate frustration.

**Structure:**
1. Acknowledge the struggle: "If you've tried [X], [Y], [Z] and still don't have [result]..."
2. Name the villain: "The real problem isn't [what they think it is] — it's [deeper root cause]"
3. Call out false solutions: "The [industry's] typical approach fails because..."
4. Deepen the mechanism: explain why the mechanism solves the root cause
5. Tease PLC-3: "Tomorrow I'll show you exactly how [client name] went from [before] to [after] using this approach"

**Tone:** Validating, empathetic, slightly provocative
**Length:** 400–700 words
**Subject line styles:**
- "The real reason [outcome isn't happening]"
- "Stop doing [false solution] (here's why it's failing you)"
- "What nobody tells [avatar] about [topic]"

---

### PLC-3: The Transformation / Social Proof

**Goal:** Make the result feel real and achievable. Transition from curiosity to desire.

**Structure:**
1. Story open: Set the scene — who is [client], what were they struggling with, when
2. The turning point: What changed when they applied the mechanism
3. The result: Specific, measurable, recent outcome
4. Bridge to reader: "You don't need [what they think they need] to get this result — you need [mechanism]"
5. Seed the offer: "I've built a full system around this. I'll share details soon."

**Tone:** Story-driven, concrete, emotionally resonant
**Length:** 500–800 words
**Subject line styles:**
- "How [client first name] went from [before state] to [after state] in [timeframe]"
- "The [mechanism] case study: [specific result]"
- "[Client first name]'s story (this will change how you think about [topic])"

## Open Cart Sequence

### Purpose
Convert engaged pre-launch subscribers into buyers during the cart open window.

### OC-1: The Announcement Email (Day 0 — Cart Opens)

**Goal:** Full offer reveal. Maximum excitement. Drive first-wave buyers.

**Structure:**
1. One-sentence hook that references the PLC journey: "For the past two weeks I've been showing you [mechanism]. Today, I'm opening the doors to the full system."
2. Offer name and core promise (what they will be able to do / achieve)
3. What's included: core program + bonus stack (with values)
4. Price and payment options
5. Deadline: "Cart closes [day] at [time]"
6. CTA: "Click here to join [offer name] →"
7. P.S.: Reinforce the #1 transformation, not a feature

**Tone:** Celebratory, clear, confident
**Length:** 600–1,000 words
**Subject line styles:**
- "The doors are open — [offer name] is here"
- "It's time. [Offer name] is now available."
- "[Mechanism]: the full system is now open"

---

### OC-2: Objection Handling + FAQ (Day 1–2)

**Goal:** Remove the top 3 reasons people are hesitating.

**Structure:**
1. Open with empathy: "I've gotten a lot of questions about [offer name]..."
2. Address objection 1: "Do I need [X] to get started? No, here's why..."
3. Address objection 2: "What if [common fear]? Here's exactly what happens..."
4. Address objection 3: "Is [price] worth it? Let's do the math..."
5. Reinforce the guarantee: "And remember, you're completely protected by [guarantee]"
6. Light deadline reminder: "You have until [deadline] to decide"
7. CTA: link to sales page

**Common objections for high-ticket programs:**
- "I don't have time" → "This is built for busy [avatars] — here's the time commitment"
- "I'm not sure it will work for me" → "Let me show you three people like you..."
- "I can't afford it" → "Here's the cost of NOT solving this..."
- "I need to think about it" → "That's fair. But here's what happens after [deadline]..."

**Tone:** Helpful, direct, non-pushy
**Length:** 500–800 words

---

### OC-3: Proof / Case Study Email (Day 2–3)

**Goal:** Social proof to convert fence-sitters who need more evidence.

**Structure:**
1. Lead with the result: "[Client name] [specific outcome] in [timeframe]"
2. Tell the story: before state, turning point, mechanism applied, result
3. Make it relatable: "What made [client]'s situation feel impossible at first..."
4. Bridge to offer: "This is exactly what [offer name] is designed to give you"
5. Deadline CTA: "You can still join until [deadline]"

**Use:** Real client case study or testimonial. If none available, use a detailed before/after scenario clearly labeled as illustrative.

---

### OC-4: Deadline Warning (Day 3–4, 48 hours before close)

**Goal:** Wake up passive list members. Real urgency.

**Structure:**
1. Subject line is the message: "[48 hours] — [Offer name] closes [day]"
2. One paragraph: what closes, when, what they lose
3. Restate the transformation promise in one sentence
4. Restate the price and bonuses in three bullet points
5. CTA: "If you're ready → [link]"
6. No fluff, no stories — pure action email

**Length:** Under 300 words

## Close Sequence

### Purpose
Capture all remaining conversions in the final 24–48 hours. The close window should drive 30–40% of total launch revenue.

### CL-1: Early Warning (48 Hours Before Deadline)

**Goal:** Final warning to fence-sitters who have not bought yet.

**Structure:**
1. Subject: "48 hours. Then [offer name] closes."
2. State exactly what closes: cart, price, bonuses
3. One sentence transformation reminder
4. Deadline with specific time and timezone
5. CTA

**Length:** Under 250 words
**Tone:** Calm, factual, confident

---

### CL-2: Final Day Email (Morning of Close Day)

**Goal:** Maximum urgency. Convert same-day decision makers.

**Structure:**
1. Subject: "[First name], today is the last day"
2. State: "Tonight at [time], [offer name] closes."
3. Final recap: what they get, what it costs, what happens after deadline
4. Optional: "I've opened [X] spots in the final 12 hours for anyone on the fence — if you want a 10-minute call to make sure this is right for you, reply to this email"
5. Strong CTA

**Length:** 300–500 words
**Tone:** Direct, warm, urgent

---

### CL-3: Last Hour Email (60 Minutes Before Deadline)

**Goal:** Final push. Short. Direct. No fluff.

**Structure:**
1. Subject: "60 minutes. [Offer name] closes tonight."
2. Two sentences: Cart closes at [time]. After that, [what happens].
3. One line on the transformation
4. Link

**Length:** Under 150 words
**Tone:** Matter-of-fact, no drama

## Email Performance Benchmarks

| Metric | Below Average | Target | Strong |
|--------|--------------|--------|--------|
| Open rate (warm list) | <20% | 25–35% | >40% |
| Open rate (cold/new) | <15% | 18–25% | >30% |
| Click rate | <2% | 3–5% | >6% |
| Unsubscribe rate per email | >1% | <0.5% | <0.2% |
| Revenue per email (launch) | Varies | 1–3% of list value | >5% of list value |

## Subject Line Principles

1. **Curiosity gap:** "The [number] thing I stopped doing (and my results tripled)"
2. **Specific result:** "How [client] got [specific metric] in [timeframe]"
3. **Direct urgency:** "48 hours. Then it's gone."
4. **Bold claim with proof:** "[Number] people did this. [Result]."
5. **Pain amplifier:** "Still struggling with [pain]? Read this."
6. **Deadline anchor:** "[Date]. Cart closes."

Avoid:
- Click-bait that does not deliver on the promise
- Generic motivational subject lines with no hook
- All-caps or excessive punctuation (triggers spam filters)
- More than one question mark or exclamation point per subject
