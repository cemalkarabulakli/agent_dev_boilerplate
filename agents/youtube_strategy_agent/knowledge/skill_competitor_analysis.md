# Skill: Competitor Analysis

Source: Serkan Turanlı Method — "YouTube'da Rakip Analizi Nasıl Yapılır?" framework
Reference: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wLEKsNr5I4I

## When to Activate This Skill

Use this skill:
- Before building a new channel (niche validation phase)
- Before creating the first content calendar
- When channel growth stalls despite consistent uploads
- When entering a new content sub-category
- Monthly maintenance: scan competitors for new gaps

## The 6-Step Competitor Analysis Process

### Step 1: Identify Competitor Channels

Search your primary niche keyword on YouTube. Collect the top 10–15 channels that appear in:
- Search results for primary keyword
- Suggested videos on your niche's top videos
- "Channels" tab in YouTube search results

**Qualify each channel by:**
- Is this a direct competitor? (same niche, same audience language, similar channel size)
- Exclude: mega-channels (10M+ subscribers) unless you're specifically studying their content gaps
- Exclude: completely different formats (podcasts, live streams only) if not your format

**Tools:**
- YouTube search (manual)
- vidIQ "Competitors" tab (tracks automatically after setup)
- Social Blade (channel size and growth history)

### Step 2: Channel Audit (Per Competitor)

For each competitor channel, document:

| Metric | Where to Find | What It Tells You |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Subscriber count | Channel page | Audience size / authority |
| Total views | Social Blade | Content longevity and scale |
| Upload frequency | Channel > Videos tab | Cadence they're committing to |
| Average views per video | Calculate: total views / video count | Views-to-sub ratio efficiency |
| Views-to-subscriber ratio | (Avg views / sub count) × 100 | Content-algorithm health score |
| Top 5 performing videos | Sorted by "Most Popular" | What the algorithm rewards |
| Worst 5 videos | Sorted by "Oldest" or "Most Recent" | What doesn't work in this niche |
| Content categories | Scan video titles | Topic clusters they focus on |
| Thumbnail style | Visual scan | Design patterns in the niche |

**Views-to-subscriber ratio benchmark:**
- Below 10%: Channel has authority but poor organic reach — strong opportunity
- 10–30%: Average channel health
- 30%+: Strong algorithm performance — study their content formula

### Step 3: Keyword Gap Analysis

For each competitor channel:
1. Open their channel in vidIQ or TubeBuddy
2. Check their "Top Keywords" (vidIQ Channel Audit feature)
3. Note keywords they rank for that you haven't targeted yet
4. Search each keyword on YouTube — is there an underserved search intent?

**Gap identification method:**
- Search their best-performing video's keyword yourself — do the top results have weak thumbnails, old publish dates, or poor SEO?
- That is your entry point.

### Step 4: Outlier Video Detection

An outlier video is any video that has 3x or more the channel's average views. Outliers reveal what the YouTube algorithm loves in your niche.

For each competitor, find their top 3 outlier videos:

**Outlier analysis checklist:**
- [ ] What is the exact title format? (Number? Question? Curiosity gap?)
- [ ] What makes the thumbnail different from their other videos?
- [ ] What topic specifically is it about — is this a repeatable topic category?
- [ ] How long is it? Is the length different from their typical videos?
- [ ] When was it published? Is it still getting views (evergreen) or was it a trend spike?

**Action:** Add outlier topic categories to your content calendar with your own SEO spin.

### Step 5: Content Gap Mapping

Content gaps are topics the audience in your niche is searching for but no top channel covers well.

**How to find content gaps:**
1. YouTube autocomplete: type your primary keyword + every letter of the alphabet — collect all suggestions
2. "People also ask" in Google for your niche keyword
3. Reddit / Quora: what questions does your niche's audience ask repeatedly?
4. Comment sections: what are viewers asking in comments of competitor videos?
5. vidIQ "Daily Ideas" feed: shows trending keywords in your niche

**Content gap qualification:**
- Is there search volume for this topic? (confirm with vidIQ)
- Does the current YouTube search result for this topic have weak, old, or poorly-SEO'd videos?
- Can you produce this content better than what exists?

### Step 6: Competitive Intelligence Summary

Compile findings into an actionable document:

```
COMPETITOR ANALYSIS SUMMARY — [NICHE] — [DATE]

Top Competitors Analyzed: [list 5–10 channels]

Strongest Competitor: [channel name]
- Weakness: [what they do poorly]
- Opportunity: [how you exploit it]

Keyword Gaps Found: [list 5–10 underserved keywords]

Outlier Topic Categories: [list 3–5 topic formats that consistently overperform]

Content Calendar Additions (next 4 weeks):
- Week 1: [Gap video 1 — keyword]
- Week 2: [Gap video 2 — keyword]
- Week 3: [Outlier replication — topic]
- Week 4: [Gap video 3 — keyword]

Thumbnail Style Insights: [what thumbnail patterns dominate this niche]

Competitive Risk: [any channel so dominant in a subtopic that competing is futile?]
```

## Competitor Analysis Tools Reference

| Tool | Free Tier Useful? | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **vidIQ** | Yes (limited) | Keyword research, competitor tracking, channel audit |
| **TubeBuddy** | Yes (limited) | SEO score, keyword explorer, A/B thumbnail test |
| **Social Blade** | Yes | Subscriber/view history, channel growth rate |
| **YouTube Studio** | Yes (your channel only) | Your own performance data |
| **YouTube Search (manual)** | Yes | Search volume proxy, autocomplete gaps, result quality |
| **Google Trends** | Yes | Seasonal demand, topic trajectory |

## Competitor Analysis Frequency

| Channel Stage | Frequency | Depth |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pre-launch | Once (deep) | Full 6-step process on 10 channels |
| 0–1,000 subs | Monthly | 3–5 channels, focus on gaps |
| 1,000–10,000 subs | Monthly | 5 channels, outlier analysis focus |
| 10,000+ subs | Bi-monthly | 3 core competitors, trend monitoring |

## Output from This Skill

After completing competitor analysis, produce:
1. Updated content calendar (4 weeks minimum)
2. Keyword gap list (minimum 10 validated keywords)
3. Outlier topic category list (minimum 3)
4. Thumbnail style guide update
5. List of competitors to continue monitoring
