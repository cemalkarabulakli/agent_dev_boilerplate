# YouTube Algorithm — How It Works

Source: YouTube Creator Academy, YouTube Engineering Blog, Serkan Turanlı Method

## The Two-Stage Algorithm

YouTube's recommendation engine works in two stages:

```
Candidate Generation → Ranking → Delivery
```

1. **Candidate Generation**: Hundreds of millions of videos narrowed to hundreds of candidates for a specific viewer, based on watch history, search history, and topical signals.
2. **Ranking**: Candidates scored by predicted satisfaction — a function of CTR probability, watch time probability, and post-watch satisfaction signals (likes, shares, surveys).

**Key implication:** A video must first be picked as a candidate (topic/keyword relevance) before ranking matters. SEO determines candidacy. Quality determines ranking.

## Primary Performance Signals

### 1. Click-Through Rate (CTR)
- Definition: Impressions that resulted in a click (%)
- Industry benchmarks: 2–10% (healthy: 4–6%)
- Driven by: thumbnail quality + title relevance + viewer intent match
- Below 4%: YouTube stops serving the video broadly
- Algorithm test window: first 24–48 hours of publishing

### 2. Average View Duration (AVD) / Audience Retention
- Definition: Average percentage of the video watched
- Healthy benchmark: 40–60% for most formats
- Below 40%: algorithm interprets as low value
- Retention curve matters: drops in the first 30 seconds are the most damaging
- "Absolute audience retention" (# of viewers staying) weighted more than average

### 3. Session Continuity
- YouTube rewards videos that keep viewers on the platform after watching
- End screens, cards, and playlists drive session continuity
- Recommended videos that continue viewing sessions get preferential treatment

## How YouTube Distributes Content

| Traffic Source | What It Means | How to Optimize |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Browse Features** | YouTube pushes video to logged-in user homepage | Strong CTR + proven watch time track record |
| **Suggested Video** | Appears next to or after another video | Topic/keyword overlap with high-performing videos |
| **Search** | Viewer actively searched for the topic | Keyword-optimized title, description, tags |
| **External** | Traffic from Google, social media, etc. | Not algorithm-driven — earned externally |
| **Notifications** | Sent to subscribers | Consistent publishing builds subscriber loyalty |

**Turanlı Method priority:** Build for Suggested and Browse. Search is the entry point; Suggested and Browse is where channels scale.

## The 48-Hour Launch Window

Every video is tested by YouTube in the first 24–48 hours against a small sample audience. YouTube observes:
- CTR on the test audience
- Retention curve
- Engagement signals (likes, comments, shares)

If signals are strong: YouTube expands distribution.
If signals are weak: distribution stops. The video enters the long tail.

**Implication:** Publishing cadence consistency and pre-publish SEO review are not optional.

## What the Algorithm Does NOT Care About

- Subscriber count (new channels with great CTR+AVD outperform large channels with poor metrics)
- Publish frequency for its own sake (quality beats quantity)
- Video length (match length to content, not to a length target)
- Upload time of day (matters marginally for the first few hours)

## Algorithm Alignment Checklist

Before publishing any video:
- [ ] Is the title keyword-validated? (will this video be found by search?)
- [ ] Is the thumbnail compelling enough to earn a 4%+ CTR from a cold audience?
- [ ] Does the first 30 seconds deliver on the title AND tease future value?
- [ ] Does the retention curve stay above 40%?
- [ ] Are end screens configured to drive session continuity?
- [ ] Is the video placed in a relevant playlist?
