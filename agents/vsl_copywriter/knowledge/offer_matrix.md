# Offer Matrix

Source: VSL Copywriter Agent — Offer Selection Framework

## Stage Assessment

### Beginner
- 0 paid clients
- Theoretical knowledge only, or learned from courses
- Has helped people for free or friends/family only
- Not yet confident in own value

### Intermediate
- Has paid clients, but unpredictably
- Has delivered results, but no consistent flow
- Has some testimonials, but not enough
- Knows the work, unsure about the business

### Proven
- Many paid clients
- Visible results, testimonials, reputation
- Established in the market

---

## Offer Matrix by Stage

### If BEGINNER — 3 offer options:

**Option A — Free-for-result**
- Type: Pro bono with outcome agreement
- Price: Free (or % on result)
- Purpose: Gather 3-5 case studies, testimonials, referrals
- Includes: Full delivery, detailed tracking of result
- When to recommend: Zero paid clients, needs proof before charging

**Option B — Small paid (10-20% of price threshold)**
- Type: Short challenge or test drive (3-7 days)
- Price: Low entry (proves value, builds experience)
- Purpose: Start collecting evidence, test delivery model
- Includes: Short intensive format, clear quick win deliverable
- When to recommend: Has helped people but never charged

**Option C — Medium (40-60% of price threshold)**
- Type: Structured 4-6 week program
- Price: Mid-range
- Purpose: First real program with a full process
- Includes: Weekly sessions, clear milestones, simple curriculum
- When to recommend: Has some proof, wants to build a real offer

---

### If INTERMEDIATE — 3 offer options:

**Option A — Low entry (~15-25% of price threshold)**
- Type: Challenge, sprint, or intensive (5-14 days)
- Price: Low barrier to entry
- Purpose: Fill pipeline, generate new testimonials, build list
- Includes: High-density short format, fast result promise
- When to recommend: Needs more social proof and a faster sales cycle

**Option B — Medium (~50-70% of price threshold)**
- Type: Accelerated program or test drive (3-4 weeks)
- Price: Mid-range
- Purpose: 1-on-1 or small group, hands-on delivery
- Includes: Sessions + accountability + specific deliverable
- When to recommend: Has proven delivery, wants to charge more

**Option C — High ticket (~100% of price threshold)**
- Type: Full program or done-for-you (2-3 months)
- Price: At or near price threshold
- Purpose: Full transformation, premium positioning
- Includes: Full access, personalized delivery, results guarantee frame
- When to recommend: Has strong proof, wants to go premium

---

### If PROVEN — 3 offer options:

**Option A — Medium (~30-50% of price threshold)**
- Type: Group program or academy
- Price: Lower, high volume
- Purpose: Scale via group delivery
- Includes: Curriculum, community, group coaching
- When to recommend: Wants to serve more people with less time

**Option B — High ticket (~70-100% of price threshold)**
- Type: 1-on-1 or small group, high engagement
- Price: Near threshold
- Purpose: Premium, exclusive positioning
- Includes: Direct access, personalized attention, strong guarantee
- When to recommend: Wants to protect time while charging premium

**Option C — Premium (~150-200% of price threshold)**
- Type: VIP, done-for-you, or exclusive model
- Price: Above threshold (push comfort zone intentionally)
- Purpose: Ultra-premium, very few clients
- Includes: Full done-for-you, full access, elite result contract
- When to recommend: Strong reputation, limited spots, premium market

---

## Naming Rules

- Name offers after the result or transformation, not "Program 1" or generic labels.
- Examples: "Financial Freedom in 90 Days", "Breakthrough Sprint", "Sales Architect"
- Match the name to the niche language — what would the ideal client call their goal?

## Pricing Rules

- Always anchor pricing to the price threshold from Question 7.
- Do not push above 100% of threshold on the first offer for a Beginner.
- For Proven stage, pushing to 150-200% is intentional and justified.
- Present realistic market-level prices. Do not invent unrealistic numbers.
