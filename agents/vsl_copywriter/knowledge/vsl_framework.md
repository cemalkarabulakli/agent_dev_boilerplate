# VSL Script Framework

Source: VSL Copywriter Agent — Internal Framework

## Purpose

A VSL (Video Sales Letter) for this funnel does NOT directly sell the offer. It sells a free consultation application. The goal is one action: click the button and fill out the form.

Target length: 3-5 minutes of spoken content.

---

## Script Structure

### 1. HOOK (2-3 sentences)

Start with a provocation, paradox, or concrete result. Frame it for the exact ideal client.

Template: "If you are [ideal client] and [pain] — the next 4 minutes will change the way you think about [topic]."

Rules:
- Must reference the specific pain or situation, not a generic problem.
- No corporate language. Speak as a person, not a brand.
- The hook must make the ideal client say "that's me" within 10 seconds.

---

### 2. FOR WHOM (30 seconds)

Qualify the viewer. Make non-ideal clients self-select out.

Template: "If you recognize this feeling [specific pain], if you've tried [alternative], but [why it doesn't work] — keep watching."

Rules:
- Name exactly one type of person with exactly one problem.
- Reference alternatives they have already tried and why those failed.
- Do not try to appeal to everyone.

---

### 3. WHY NOTHING WORKS (45 seconds)

List 2-3 reasons why standard solutions fail. Concrete, not generic.

Rules:
- Each reason must explain a mechanism, not just "it doesn't work."
- Must resonate with the specific niche — not generic self-help problems.
- Sets up the contrast for the next section.

---

### 4. WHAT IS DIFFERENT (45 seconds)

Explain the unique mechanism of this person's approach. Not "I do it better" — the WHY it works mechanically.

Rules:
- Must be specific to the expert's actual method from Phase 1 answers.
- Avoid superlatives. Explain the mechanism.
- Do not claim to be the only solution — explain why this specific approach addresses the root cause.

---

### 5. PROOF (30 seconds)

One concrete example from Question 3 (real client, real result).

Template: "[Client type X] came to me with [specific problem]. We did [specific step]. The result was [specific measurable result]."

Rules:
- One example, real, with real numbers or observable outcomes.
- Never invent results.
- If no paid clients yet — use a friend/colleague example and label it as such.

---

### 6. CALL TO ACTION (30 seconds)

Invite to click the button and apply for a free consultation.

Template: "If you want to discuss how this could happen for you — click the button below. I'll ask you a few questions to understand if I can help. If yes — you'll book a short call with me. No promises, no pressure. Just a conversation."

Rules:
- Reduce friction: no promises, no pressure.
- Name the next step clearly: click → fill form → book call.
- Do not oversell the consultation itself.

---

### 7. SCARCITY (15 seconds)

Real limitation on consultation slots.

Template: "I only have time for [X] conversations this month. If you see yourself in what I said — act now."

Rules:
- Only use real scarcity. Do not invent fake urgency.
- If no real limit exists — use a soft version: "I prefer to work with a small number of people at a time, to be able to deliver a real result."

---

## Tone Rules

- Direct and warm. Not corporate, not coach-speak.
- No exclamation marks used for empty motivation.
- No "amazing!", "incredible!", "life-changing!" unless describing a real result.
- Speak to one person, not a crowd.
- Match the language register of the niche (professional vs. casual).
