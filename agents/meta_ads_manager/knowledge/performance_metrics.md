# Performance Metrics, KPIs, and Creative Fatigue Detection

## Core Metrics

| Metric | Formula | What It Measures |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ROAS | Revenue ÷ Ad Spend | Direct revenue efficiency |
| CPA | Ad Spend ÷ Conversions | Cost to acquire one customer/lead |
| CPM | (Ad Spend ÷ Impressions) × 1,000 | Audience competition / saturation |
| CTR | (Clicks ÷ Impressions) × 100 | Ad relevance and hook effectiveness |
| Hook Rate | 3-second views ÷ Impressions × 100 | First-impression quality — #1 creative health signal |
| Frequency | Impressions ÷ Reach | How many times same user sees the ad |
| CVR | Conversions ÷ Clicks × 100 | Landing page / offer quality |
| CPC | Ad Spend ÷ Clicks | Click cost efficiency |

## Andromeda-Era Creative Fatigue Framework

In the Andromeda era, creative fatigue arrives in **2–3 weeks** (vs. 4–6 weeks pre-Andromeda). Andromeda saturates your viable audience faster because it is more precise at matching the same creative to the same people.

### The 5 Fatigue Signals (ranked by detection speed)

1. **Hook Rate < 25%** — Catches fatigue 2–3 days early. This is the leading indicator.
2. **CTR declining 5+ consecutive days** — Persistent decline; 3–5 day advance warning.
3. **Frequency > 3.0 in cold prospecting** — Real-time saturation alert.
4. **CPM stable + CTR dropping simultaneously** — Most reliable signal. Audience disengaging, not auction pressure.
5. **Creative age > 21 days without refresh review** — Preventive; risk indicator before fatigue is measurable.

## Kill vs. Optimize vs. Wait Decision Matrix

### Kill the Ad Immediately
- Spent > 1× AOV with zero conversions (zombie ad)
- Hook Rate < 10% with spend > $50
- CPA > 3× target with 50+ conversions tested
- Frequency > 4.0 in cold prospecting with no improvement

### Optimize (Not Kill)
- Good hook rate but poor CVR → landing page issue, not the ad
- CPA elevated but CTR improving → offer or copy adjustment needed
- Strong 3-sec view rate but engagement drops at 10 seconds → content after hook is weak

### Wait (Do Nothing)
- Campaign still in learning phase (< 50 conversions)
- Budget changed in the last 72 hours
- Creative launched less than 24 hours ago
- 1–2 fatigue signals activating (monitor for 5–7 days before acting)

## Automated Alert Rules

Trigger creative refresh when:
- CTR down 20% week-over-week AND Frequency > 3.0
- Hook Rate below 15% for 3+ consecutive days
- CPA up 20%+ from 7-day baseline for 7+ consecutive days

Trigger signal quality review when:
- Cost per result increasing without corresponding CPM increase
- ROAS declining week-over-week with no creative fatigue signals (usually CAPI issue)

## Diagnosing CPA Increases

When CPA rises above target, diagnose in this order:

1. **Is the campaign in learning phase?** → If yes, wait. Do not optimize.
2. **Is CAPI EMQ still above 7.0?** → If no, fix signal first. More spend on degraded signal = worse results.
3. **Is frequency above 3.0 in cold prospecting?** → If yes, creative fatigue is the cause. Refresh portfolio.
4. **Is Hook Rate dropping?** → If yes, creative problem. Audit and replace weakest hooks.
5. **Is CTR stable but CVR dropping?** → Landing page issue or offer mismatch. Not an ads problem.
6. **Is CPM spiking?** → Audience saturation or increased competition. Expand audience or test new creative angles.

## Weekly Performance Review Checklist

Use this in the weekly Andromeda flywheel Step 2 (Analyze):

- [ ] Hook Rate for all active creatives (kill anything below 15%)
- [ ] CTR trend over past 7 days (declining = flag for refresh queue)
- [ ] Frequency by audience segment (above 3.0 in cold = act)
- [ ] CPA vs. target for past 7 days
- [ ] ROAS vs. target for past 7 days
- [ ] Creative age audit (anything older than 21 days → review for fatigue)
- [ ] P.D.A. portfolio balance check (are new concepts testing?)
- [ ] Learning phase status for all active campaigns
- [ ] CAPI EMQ score (flag if below 7.0)
